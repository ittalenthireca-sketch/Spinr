## Independent Contractor Agreement

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into
as of [DATE] between:

- **Spinr Technologies Inc.** ("Spinr"), a corporation incorporated
  under the laws of [Province], and
- **[DRIVER FULL LEGAL NAME]** ("Contractor"), an individual
  residing at [ADDRESS].

---

## 1. Nature of Relationship

1.1 Contractor is an **independent contractor**, not an employee,
agent, partner, or joint venturer of Spinr.

1.2 Spinr does not control the manner, means, hours, or timing of
Contractor's work. Contractor sets their own schedule, may decline
any ride request, and may simultaneously use other transportation
network platforms.

1.3 Contractor acknowledges that they will not receive employee
benefits including vacation pay, sick leave, statutory holiday pay,
overtime pay, Canada Pension Plan employer contributions, Employment
Insurance employer premiums, or Workers' Compensation coverage paid
by Spinr (except where provincial law mandates otherwise — Contractor
is responsible for determining their own obligations).

---

## 2. Services

2.1 Contractor agrees to use the Spinr Driver App to accept ride
requests from riders who have booked through the Spinr platform.

2.2 Contractor is responsible for:
- Providing a roadworthy motor vehicle in good repair.
- Maintaining a valid driver's licence and a current driving
  abstract at all times.
- Obtaining and maintaining commercial or ride-share-endorsed
  automobile insurance as required by provincial law.
- Complying with all applicable traffic laws, municipal bylaws,
  and provincial TNC regulations.
- Maintaining a professional and safe standard of service.

2.3 Spinr provides technology only — the app, dispatch algorithm,
payment processing, and rider interface. Spinr does not direct the
Contractor's driving route except to convey the rider's requested
destination.

---

## 3. Earnings

3.1 Contractor earns a per-trip fee based on the fare structure
described in the Spinr Driver Rate Card (published in the Driver App
and updated from time to time with 14 days' notice).

3.2 Earnings are paid weekly by direct deposit to the bank account
Contractor provides during onboarding.

3.3 Spinr deducts a **platform service fee** (the "Spinr Commission")
from each fare. The current commission rate is published in the
Driver Rate Card.

3.4 **Dynamic pricing (Surge):** Fares may be multiplied by a surge
factor during high-demand periods. Contractor earns the surged amount
less the same flat commission percentage.

3.5 **Tips:** Riders may optionally add a tip via the app. 100% of
tips are passed to the Contractor; Spinr does not charge commission
on tips.

---

## 4. Taxes

4.1 Contractor is solely responsible for all income taxes, self-
employment taxes, and GST/HST obligations arising from their earnings.

4.2 Spinr will issue a **T4A slip** (Statement of Contract Payments)
by February 28 of each year for Contractors earning $500 CAD or more
in the preceding calendar year.

4.3 Contractor is required to provide a valid Social Insurance Number
(SIN) or Business Number (BN) for T4A reporting. Failure to provide
this information may result in Spinr being required to withhold taxes.

4.4 Contractor is advised to consult a tax professional about
GST/HST registration obligations, quarterly installments, and
permissible business deductions (vehicle, phone, data, etc.).

---

## 5. Vehicle and Insurance

5.1 Contractor must maintain, at their own expense:
- A valid personal automobile insurance policy with a commercial-use
  or ride-hailing endorsement, OR a commercial automobile policy that
  covers passenger transport for compensation.
- Proof of insurance must be uploaded to the Driver App and kept
  current. A lapsed insurance certificate will result in immediate
  suspension of the Contractor's driving privileges on the platform.

5.2 Spinr maintains a contingent commercial liability policy that
provides coverage when Contractor is actively transporting a rider
and the Contractor's personal policy does not apply. Details are
posted in the Driver App under "Insurance."

---

## 6. Background Checks and Ongoing Compliance

6.1 Contractor consents to an initial criminal record check and motor
vehicle record (MVR) check as a condition of activation.

6.2 Spinr may request a refreshed MVR or criminal record check
annually or in response to a safety incident.

6.3 Contractor must promptly notify Spinr of any criminal charge,
conviction, or driving prohibition that would affect their eligibility
to transport riders.

---

## 7. Data and Privacy

7.1 Contractor consents to the collection of GPS location data
during active rides for purposes of dispatch, safety, and dispute
resolution. Data is retained per Spinr's Data Retention Policy
(`docs/compliance/DATA_RETENTION.md`).

7.2 Contractor's earnings records and SIN/BN are encrypted at rest
and accessible only to authorized Spinr personnel for tax-reporting
purposes.

---

## 8. Deactivation

8.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, without
cause, with 24 hours' notice.

8.2 Spinr may immediately deactivate a Contractor's account (without
notice) in the event of:
- A safety incident involving a rider.
- A failed background check.
- Fraudulent activity.
- Violation of applicable law or Spinr's Community Guidelines.

8.3 Contractor may appeal a deactivation by emailing
support@spinr.app within 14 days.

---

## 9. Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising from this Agreement shall first be submitted to
mediation administered by a mutually agreed mediator. If mediation
fails, disputes shall be resolved by arbitration under the
Arbitration Act of [Province]. Nothing in this clause prevents
either party from seeking urgent injunctive relief.

---

[Saskatchewan / Alberta / other] and the laws of Canada applicable
therein.

## 11. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with the Spinr Driver Rate Card and
Community Guidelines (incorporated by reference), constitutes the
entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior
negotiations, representations, or agreements.
